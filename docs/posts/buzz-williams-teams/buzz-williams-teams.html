What Defines a Buzz Williams Team? A look through Virginia Tech, Texas A&M, and What It Means for Maryland

Author

Tahlia Williams

Published

November 21, 2025

Buzz Williams has coached 18 seasons as a division I head coach, with recent stops at Virginia Tech (2014-2019) and Texas A&M (2019-2024). Using NCAA game logs from 2015 to present, I analyzed every game he coached in that span to identify what actually defines a Buzz Williams team. Rather than focusing on wins and losses, I wanted to understand the style, statistical identity, and the consistent performance patterns accross different schools and rosters. Then, I compared those traits to Maryland’s performance over the past two seasons to determine how this may shape Maryland’s future.

buzz <- basketball |>
  filter(
    (Team == "Virginia Tech" & Season %in% c("2014-2015","2015-2016","2016-2017","2017-2018","2018-2019")) |
    (Team == "Texas A&M" & Season %in% c("2019-2020","2020-2021","2021-2022","2022-2023","2023-2024","2024-2025"))
  )

Looking back at Buzz Williams’ teams at Virginia Tech and Texas A&M you can see they have a really clear identity. His teams are built on speed or flash as well as toughness, discipline, and consistency. Defensively, they slow opponents down keeping games in the 60s and low 70s, and force teams into difficult, contested shots without gambling for steals. At Texas A&M, his teams made a living on effort that they were some of the best offensive-rebounding teams in the country, constantly creating second chances and wearing opponents down over 40 minutes. Offensively, Williams shows that he likes structure and patience. His teams play deliberately, focusing on drawing a lot of fouls, and relying on efficient two-point scoring even during seasons when the three-point shooting wasn’t there. However, his teams can be turnover-prone, and their three-point shooting tends to come and go.

When you compare that to what Maryland has looked like the past couple seasons, they have already played slower than most of the Big Ten, defends at a high level, and has dealt with similar issues of scoring droughts, turnover problems, and inconsistent perimeter shooting.
